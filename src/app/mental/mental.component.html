Accumulating successes

One way to enhance your self-confidence is by accumulating successes. To do this, create a personal "success bank" where you can store your accomplishments. Every night before bed, take a moment to reflect on the day's training and identify one thing you did well. Add that success to your bank and let it play in your mind as you drift off to sleep. This simple practice can help you focus on your achievements and build a strong sense of self-confidence over time.

One way to build self-confidence is to collect successes.

The successes you collect can vary in size and significance, but the important thing is that the value of your success bank should increase each day. Whenever you find yourself being overwhelmed by negative thoughts, you can refer to your success bank for a boost of self-confidence. One particularly beneficial time to review the contents of your success bank is in the week leading up to a tournament, when self-doubt and uncertainty can start to creep in. This can help to remind yourself of your capabilities and achievements, and give you the confidence you need to perform at your best.

When you find yourself feeling anxious or stressed about an upcoming event, try to take a step back and focus on the successes you've accumulated in your personal bank. Recall those memories in as much detail as possible, and allow yourself to relive them. If you find yourself hearing negative self-talk such as "You can't do it", reach for one of your successes to counteract those thoughts. These key moments are when your past accomplishments can provide the most powerful motivation and self-assurance. Remind yourself, "I remember that moment when..." and let your successes speak louder than your doubts.

Instead of holding onto negative experiences or unsuccessful snooker practice sessions, it's best to let them go and instead focus on positive experiences and moments. This can help improve overall performance and mindset in the snooker.

Act as if it already is true

Another way to boost confidence is to adopt the mindset of someone who already possesses it. This means consistently embodying the attitude of a self-assured individual, both in thought and action. Even when feeling uncertain, by pretending to be confident, it can ultimately lead to an internal shift in how one perceives themselves. This can bring about a positive change in self-perception.

By pretending until it becomes true, you can instill confidence within yourself, even when feeling uncertain internally.
